ℹ️ How to Use This Book

This Noorani Qaida teaches you to read the Quran from the very beginning. Start from Lesson 1 and go in order. Each lesson builds on the previous one. Read each Arabic letter and example aloud. Practice until you are confident before moving to the next lesson. Consistency is key — even 15 minutes a day will produce excellent results within weeks.

Long Vowels (Huroof Maddah)
حروف مدّہ
📌 Key Rule Long vowels are formed by combining a short vowel with a vowel letter. They are held for two counts (two beats). There are three long vowels: long "aa", long "ii", and long "uu".

Long "uu" اُ → بُو "uu" — like "moon" نُورٌ Light

6
Sukoon (Rest — No Vowel Sound)
سکون
📌 Key Rule The sukoon (ـْ) is a small circle placed above a letter. It means that letter carries no vowel — just the pure consonant sound. The letter stops dead without any "a", "i", or "u" after it.
بَيْتٌ
bayt (house)
عِلْمٌ
ʿilm (knowledge)
قَلْبٌ
qalb (heart)
مِنْ
min (from)
✅ Practice Tip When you see sukoon, say the consonant quickly and move to the next letter. Do not add any extra sound. Example: مَلَكٌ (malaK + un) — the ك has sukoon… wait, that has tanwin. Better: بِنْتٌ = b-i-n-t (bint = girl).

10
Rules of Noon Saakin & Tanwin
نون ساکن و تنوین کے احکام
📌 Key Rule When a still noon (نْ) or tanwin appears before another letter, it changes its sound based on which letter follows. There are four main rules: Idghaam, Ikhfaa, Iqlaab, and Idhhaar.

1. Idhhaar — Clear Noon

مِنْ حَيْثُ

Before throat letters (ء ه ع غ ح خ) — pronounce noon clearly.

2. Idghaam — Merge Noon

مَن يَقُول

Before (ي ن م و ر ل) — noon merges into the next letter (usually with ghunnah).

3. Ikhfaa — Hidden Noon

مِن تَحْتِ

Before the remaining 15 letters — hold a nasal sound between noon and the next letter.

4. Iqlaab — Noon becomes Meem

مِن بَعْدِ

Before ب only — noon changes to a nasal "m" sound with lips slightly closed.

11
Rules of Still Meem (Meem Saakin)
میم ساکن کے احکام
📌 Key Rule When Meem (مْ) has sukoon, it has three possible pronunciations based on the letter that follows it.

Idghaam Shafawi

هُم مَعَكُم

Before م — meem merges into the next meem with a nasal sound.

Ikhfaa Shafawi

هُم بِكُلِّ

Before ب — hold lips together briefly with a hidden nasal sound.

Idhhaar Shafawi

عَلَيْهِمْ وَ

Before all other letters — pronounce meem clearly and quickly.

12
Recitation Stop Signs (Waqf)
علامات وقف
📌 Key Rule Stop signs (waqf) guide the reciter when to stop, pause, or continue. Stopping correctly is essential for preserving the meaning of the Quran.
SignNameMeaning & Action
مWaqf LaazimMandatory stop — must stop here, meaning changes if you continue.
طWaqf MutlaqAbsolute stop — highly recommended to stop.
جWaqf Jaa'izPermissible stop — you may stop or continue.
زWaqf MujawwazPermissible but better to continue.
صWaqf MurakhkhasAllowed stop (only) due to long verse.
قليWaqf QaliStopping is preferred.
صليWasle AulaContinuing is preferred.
لاLaa Ta'qufDo not stop — stopping here distorts the meaning.
۝Verse EndEnd of an ayah — it is sunnah to stop here.

Lesson 13 — Makhaarij al-Huroof

مخارج الحروف

The articulation points of Arabic letters — where each sound originates in the mouth or throat

① Al-Jawf (Hollow / Open Air)

ا و ي

Long vowel letters — sound comes from open air in mouth/throat.

② Al-Halq (Throat)

ء ه ع غ ح خ

Six letters from different parts of the throat — deep, guttural sounds.

③ Al-Lisaan (Tongue)

ق ك ج ش ي ص ز س ض ل ن ر ط د ت ظ ذ ث

18 letters from different positions of the tongue — most Arabic sounds.

④ Ash-Shafataan (Lips)

ب م و ف

Four letters from the lips — labial sounds, some involving teeth.

⑤ Al-Khayshoom (Nasal)

غنّہ

Nasal passage — used for ghunnah (nasal sound in noon/meem with shaddah).
